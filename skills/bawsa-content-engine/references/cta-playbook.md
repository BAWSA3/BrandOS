# CTA Playbook

## The Rule
Every post gets a CTA. No exceptions. This is the #1 gap (score: 45/100).

## CTA Types

### 1. RT + Follow
**When:** End of threads, shareable insights
**Format:** "RT tweet 1 if this was useful + follow for more [topic]"
**Why it works:** Double action — distributes your content AND grows followers
**Best for:** Threads, framework posts

### 2. Agree/Disagree
**When:** Hot takes, polarizing opinions
**Format:** "agree or disagree?" or "agree or disagree? I want to hear your take"
**Why it works:** Low friction to reply. Creates debate in the replies which signals engagement to the algorithm.
**Best for:** Hot takes, Wednesday posts

### 3. Reply With Yours
**When:** Behind-the-scenes, build logs, personal posts
**Format:** "reply with what you're building" / "reply with yours" / "what's your take?"
**Why it works:** Invites conversation. People love talking about themselves.
**Best for:** Build logs, Thursday posts, weekend posts

### 4. Tag Someone
**When:** Data posts, educational threads
**Format:** "tag someone who needs to see this" / "tag someone building their personal brand"
**Why it works:** Direct distribution request. Each tag = new eyeballs.
**Best for:** Data posts, Friday threads

### 5. Save This
**When:** Frameworks, checklists, how-tos
**Format:** "save this for your next [X]" / "save this if you're tired of [problem]"
**Why it works:** Saves signal value to the algorithm. Bookmarks = quality indicator.
**Best for:** Monday framework posts, actionable tips

### 6. Drop Handle
**When:** Community engagement, connection posts
**Format:** "drop your handle below" / "drop it below"
**Why it works:** High reply volume. Creates community feel. Reply count boosts visibility.
**Best for:** Saturday community posts

### 7. Quote RT
**When:** Reflections, broad takes, opinion posts
**Format:** "quote RT with your biggest [X]" / "quote this with your version"
**Why it works:** Quote RTs distribute your content to the quoter's entire audience.
**Best for:** Sunday reflections, mid-week takes

### 8. Reply Trigger (Value Offer)
**When:** Once per week maximum
**Format:** "reply 'audit' and I'll [do something valuable for you]"
**Why it works:** Creates massive reply volume. Builds DM relationships. Shows expertise.
**Best for:** Wednesday Post 2 or end-of-week offers
**Warning:** Only offer what you can actually deliver. Don't overcommit.

## Weekly CTA Rotation

Avoid using the same CTA type 3 days in a row. Suggested rotation:

| Day | Post 1 CTA | Post 2 CTA |
|-----|-----------|-----------|
| Mon | Save This | Reply With Yours |
| Tue | RT + Follow | Quote RT |
| Wed | Agree/Disagree | Reply Trigger (or Reply) |
| Thu | Reply With Yours | Save This |
| Fri | Tag Someone | Reply With Yours |
| Sat | Drop Handle | RT + Follow |
| Sun | Quote RT | RT + Follow |

## Common Mistakes

1. **Ending with a period instead of a question.** "interesting." vs "agree or disagree?" — the question gets 5x more replies.
2. **Burying the CTA in the middle.** CTA is always the LAST line of the post.
3. **Being vague.** "let me know what you think" < "reply with your version"
4. **Asking for too much.** One CTA per post. Not "RT, follow, reply, and save."
5. **Forgetting the CTA entirely.** This is the #1 reason posts don't convert to followers.
